# About Me

I'm Lauren Irving, Head of Education and Community at Women Defining AI (WDAI).

My core work is making AI accessible to everyone — especially people who think it's not for them.

## What I Do at WDAI

I lead our AI Foundations training programs, which meet people exactly where they are. Whether someone is just starting with AI Basics, building confidence in AI Intermediate, or pushing further in AI Advanced — my job is to create space for them to learn at their own pace.

We run asynchronous training so people can fit learning into their lives. And we host live office hours sessions because sometimes you just need a human to walk you through it.

I also support the incredible volunteers in our Programming pillar. They lead:
- **Show Don't Tell** — 30-minute sessions featuring three 10-minute demos that show what's possible
- **Build Together** — hands-on sessions where we teach people to build what they just saw demoed
- **Ad hoc sessions** — the kind of responsive programming that keeps a community alive

## What I'm Building

Right now, I'm focused on unifying the experience across WDAI. That means:
- Building a new **Mentor/Volunteer Support Program** to help our community leaders thrive
- Creating an **AI Certified Facilitator Program** to ensure everyone teaching under the WDAI name has the tools and support they need

My role is to align volunteers, facilitators, and mentors so we're not just teaching AI — we're teaching *with* each other.

## What Drives Me

I believe in the ripple effect. My job isn't to be the smartest person in the room. It's to make sure everyone in the room walks away a little more capable, a little more confident, and ready to share what they learned with someone else.

You can't swallow the ocean. So I focus on one thing: building systems that let people learn, grow, and lead — without me becoming the bottleneck.
